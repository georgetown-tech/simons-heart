Cardiac Chain Of Survival<\/h1>\n<\/div>\n<\/div><\/div>
\n
\n\t

Despite our best efforts, we will never eliminate sudden cardiac arrest. We need to be prepared when it strikes. The good news is that there is a routine. It is three easy steps. Collectively, it is called the Chain of Survival.<\/p>\n

If a student collapses and is not responding or is shaking (without a pulse or breathing), assume that she is in cardiac arrest. Don\u2019t be fooled by the shaking. It is often a symptom of cardiac arrest.<\/p>\n

3 Steps in the Cardiac Chain of Survival<\/h3>\n

(1) Call 911 <\/strong>- You know how to use a phone, right? Stay calm and call 911. The operator will ask you questions and tell you what to do. Take a deep breath and listen.<\/p>\n

(2) Administer <\/strong>cardiopulmonary resuscitation<\/strong><\/a> (CPR)<\/strong> \u2013 Have you ever pumped up a ball with air? Think of that when you do this. Your hands will take over for the heart (which isn\u2019t working at the moment), and pump blood and oxygen throughout the body. This keeps the brain and other organs functioning. CPR is easy.\u00a0 It is just chest compressions. Check out this video for some entertainment and education.<\/a><\/p>\n

(3) Get an <\/strong>automated external defibrillator<\/strong><\/a> (AED)<\/strong> \u2013 This devices is a life saver! Hopefully, you will find it in a case on the wall in the hallway of your school or other facility. Turn it on. It will start talking to you. \u201cPut the pads on the patient\u2019s bare chest.\u201d Then, it will evaluate the heart and determine if a shock is needed. It will not deliver a shock unless it is necessary. So, if you remember one thing from this paragraph, turn on the device and listen!<\/p>\n

What Else You Should Know?<\/h3>\n

Your school should have a Cardiac Emergency Response Plan<\/a> (CERP) to ensure that the everyone is prepared in the event of a cardiac emergency. With a little preparation, you can make your school a safer place for students and educators alike. Don\u2019t hesitate, remember the cardiac chain of survival, and prepare yourself today.<\/p>\n

Even though cardiac arrest is deadly serious, we can have fun learning about it. See what we are doing with the Chain of Survival Relay<\/a> and CPR Jukebox<\/a>.<\/p>\n
