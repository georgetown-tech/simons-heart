        Ways To Give as an Individual<\/span><\/h1>\n<\/div>\n<\/div><\/div>
        \n

        We can't do this alone. We rely on the generosity and creativity of the community. There are plenty of ways that we can work together to protect hearts + save lives. We've listed a few ideas below! Of course, you can always make a donation<\/a> or volunteer<\/a>.<\/p>\n

        Become a High School Student Ambassador<\/span><\/h3>\n

        Do you have a high school student in your life? The Student Ambassador Program<\/a> was designed by students for students. Raise awareness about sudden cardiac arrest at your high school and participate in a variety of events.<\/p>\n

        Get a Company Match<\/span><\/h3>\n

        Does your company match funds? Does it allow you to allocate part of your salary to a United Way Campaign (Simon's Fund #45799). Will it make a donation to a nonprofit in exchange for your volunteer hours? If you have any questions about these options, please contact us.<\/a><\/p>\n

        Host a Heart Screening<\/span><\/h3>\n

        Want to bring Simon's Heart to your school? Whether you're a health professional, an educator, or a student, Simon's Heart can work with you to\u00a0host a screening.\u00a0Fill out this form<\/a>\u00a0to get started.<\/p>\n

        Plan a Community Service (Mitzvah) Project<\/span><\/h3>\n

        Kids helping kids - there is something amazing about that concept. Your child can help plan a screening, create a project, or host a fundraiser for Simon's Heart. Our students are pretty creative: They\u2019ve hosted chess tournaments, designed jewelry for sale, teamed up with Whole Foods, and more. \u00a0Please contact us with your questions and ideas.<\/p>\n

        Can't decide? Then just shoot us a message<\/a> and we'll figure it out together.<\/p>\n
