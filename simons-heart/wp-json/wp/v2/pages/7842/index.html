To save the life of one child . . . and then another, by raising awareness about conditions that lead to sudden cardiac arrest and death.<\/p>\n

We accomplish our mission by taking a DIRE<\/strong> approach.<\/p>\n

Detection<\/strong>. We need to discover these conditions before they take our children. We screen our children\u2019s eyes and ears in school, why don\u2019t we screen their hearts?<\/p>\n

Innovation<\/strong>. <\/b>There is little focus and few resources dedicated to sudden cardiac arrest in children. By leveraging technology, we can promote research <\/a>and empower communities.<\/p>\n

Reaction<\/strong>.<\/b> We need to be prepared when sudden cardiac arrest strikes. Survival rates improve dramatically when citizens know the chain of survival<\/a>, which includes 911, CPR<\/a> and an AED device<\/a>.<\/p>\n

Education<\/strong>. Sudden cardiac arrest is not just an adult thing. There are warning signs<\/a>. We need to make sure that pediatricians, coaches, parents and students know them.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div><\/div><\/div><\/div><\/div>","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
