Published in the Huffington Post<\/p>\n

\n

Another Mother\u2019s Day is in the books. It is my 44th. For thirty-one years, the day was dedicated to honoring my mother.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

In my 32nd year, we welcomed Sally, our first child. Whoa did Mother\u2019s Day change! It became so much more special and relevant because I finally knew what it meant to be a mother. With that first sleepless night and diaper change, appreciation for my mother went through the roof.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

In my 34th year, it changed again. Simon was born. Ninety-six days later he died. He was a seemingly health three-month old. However, he had an unknown heart condition. After he died, I was diagnosed with the same condition.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Now, eleven years later, I still have a hard time articulating what his death did to my Mother\u2019s Day. I don\u2019t know how to approach it. I have two beautiful kids, and my mom lives fifteen minutes down the road. There is so much to celebrate and appreciate. But…<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Most girls have thought about becoming a mother. From what I\u2019ve read, the majority of women who experience it claim that it is the most challenging and rewarding part of life. It is a role for which we are completely unprepared. Somehow, we manage.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

As I struggle to process my emotions for yet another Mother\u2019s Day season, I want to share some quotes about motherhood. Some are profound. Others are endearing. You\u2019ll recognize a few of the names. You know, the celebrities. It\u2019s fun to see how famous people view motherhood.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

However, pay closer attention to the quotes from the other women. These are women I got to know after Simon died through a group called Parent Heart Watch<\/a>. Like me, they lost children to unknown heart conditions and founded organizations to spare other families from tragedy.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Like me, they struggle to make sense of this holiday, and well, a world where you can lose your child to a detectable and treatable heart condition.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Motherhood is tough. If you just want a wonderful little creature to love, you can get a puppy.
\nBarbara Walters<\/a><\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Motherhood has completely changed me. It\u2019s just about like the most completely humbling experience that I\u2019ve ever had. I think that it puts you in your place because it really forces you to address the issues that you claim to believe in and if you can\u2019t stand up to those principles when you\u2019re raising a child, forget it.
\nDiane Keaton<\/a><\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

The natural state of motherhood is unselfishness. When you become a mother, you are no longer the center of your own universe. You relinquish that position to your children.
\nJessica Lange<\/a><\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

I never imagined the range and depth of feelings that motherhood would introduce into my life. I\u2019ve gone from one extreme of incredible pride, an abundance of love and immeasurable joy…to the unthinkable edge of despair and knock-the-wind-out-of-you total devastation after losing my precious 19 year old daughter without warning. At the best moments before my tragic loss, I remember having the feeling that my heart would burst with love and joy. And now, it\u2019s as though my heart has been completely shattered and I\u2019m not sure if the pieces can ever be reassembled.
\nJulie Walker, The Peyton Walker Foundation<\/a><\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Each of my children carry a piece of my heart but it broke into more pieces than it was made of, when my son died.
\nDarla Varrenti, Nick of Time Foundation<\/a><\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Being a mother: There is no greater gift, nor bigger heartache.
\nJeremy Lopynski, Ryan Lopynski Big Heart Foundation<\/a><\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Motherhood is a life-long promise from you to your child. You promise to protect and nurture and care for your child, to help them grow to adulthood. Sadly, despite your very best efforts, tragedy can wipe that out in a moment, and you\u2019re left shouldering the pain of a failed promise.
\nMary Beth Schewitz, Max Schewitz Foundation<\/a><\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Motherhood is the greatest gift and I\u2019m thankful God chose me. Motherhood has brought me unconditional love, hope, joy, sorrow, and laughter. Motherhood is pure beauty.
\nLaura Friend, Run for Sarah<\/a><\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

I guess these sayings are really true. You don\u2019t know what you got until it\u2019s gone. To understand, walk in another (wo)man\u2019s shoes.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

Motherhood is something that we dream about and imagine. We know there will be laughter and celebrations. We know there will be tears and heartbreak, but we think it\u2019ll be the \u201cmy boyfriend broke up with me\u201d or the \u201cI failed a test\u201d kind.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

I didn\u2019t know how to change a diaper. I didn\u2019t know all of the different kinds of formula available for reflux. I didn\u2019t know how to install a car seat. I didn\u2019t know how to bury a child. I\u2019ve learned all of these things on the fly. I\u2019ve faked it.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n

There is one thing I do know. Our children don\u2019t come with a timeline or expiration date. Their roads and destinies are unknown. I was prepared to do whatever it took to get Simon to his destination. Instead, he gave his life for me, so that I could discover my heart condition and reach my destination, whatever that may be. I\u2019m not sure that is how motherhood is supposed to work.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n
