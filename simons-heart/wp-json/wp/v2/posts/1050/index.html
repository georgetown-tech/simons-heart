<\/a>Yesterday sucked.\u00a0 Simon’s sixth birthday.\u00a0 After six years, you’d think . . . . Nothing.\u00a0 It doesn’t get easier.\u00a0 The days in between the birthday get easier, but not the birthday.\u00a0 After losing a kid, every holiday and life cycle event can’t just happen.\u00a0 Even if there is some joy in the occasion, it comes with a price.\u00a0 You need to analyze it.\u00a0 You need to justify it.\u00a0 I came across this email and think it does a nice job of conveying the perspective of a parent dealing with the loss of a child.<\/p>\n

The year after [he] died, I\u00a0knew the holidays were going to be hard. 4th of July tradition had been broken and it was hard. Labor Day when we all went\u00a0away was\u00a0harder. He loved to goof around with the kids on Halloween and that was hard too. That Thanksgiving where we all sat around the table after a big dinner telling stories was missing our biggest story teller. 30 days later for Christmas was as hard as I knew it would be. But his birthday was unbearable which for some reason I wasn’t prepared for. I realize now that the big holidays is still surrounded by family and friends. But his birthday became an empty day where once their was a celebration for just him.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
