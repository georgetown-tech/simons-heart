 <\/p>\n

Yarzheit [yar-tzhight] is the Jewish practice of remembering the death of a loved one.\u00a0 It is based on the Jewish calendar.\u00a0 In\u00a0our secular society,\u00a0this anniverary\u00a0takes a back seat to the “real” annivesary of Simon’s death,\u00a0January 24, 2005.\u00a0 Most people,\u00a0have no idea that there is this other anniversary,\u00a0the 15th day of Shvat.<\/p>\n

I attended synagogue this morning to say mourner’s kaddish, a prayer to remember\u00a0Simon.\u00a0\u00a0I was surrounded by\u00a0guests of a bat mitzvah so the overall mood was joyous.\u00a0 They were there to celebrate\u00a0the accomplishments of\u00a0a young girl.\u00a0 I was there to remember a boy, my son, that will never reach the age of 13.<\/p>\n

I couldn’t say the mourner’s kaddish.\u00a0 The words were out-muscled by the grief and tears.\u00a0 The best I could do was move my lips.\u00a0\u00a0I’ve been in a funk all day, which is remarkable considering that this is the “secondary” anniversary.\u00a0 However, today reminded me\u00a0of the significance\u00a0of\u00a0yarzheit.<\/p>\n

It is always great to receive well wishes from friends.\u00a0 When I receive emails and calls, or\u00a0friends change their profile pictures on Facebook on January 24, my spirits soar.\u00a0 This support and recognition is so therapeutic.\u00a0 However,\u00a0something magical happened today.<\/p>\n

I\u00a0stood in\u00a0front of a joyful crowd to recite a prayer\u00a0to remember my son.\u00a0 In the midst of\u00a0their celebration, they\u00a0paused to\u00a0acknowledged my grief.\u00a0 They stood with me as I tried to speak.\u00a0 They surrounded and supported me.\u00a0 In a very spiritual and physical way, they lifted me up.<\/p>\n

Community is very important.\u00a0 Lately, we\u00a0are obsessed with virtual communities, and impressed by how easy it is to connect with people from around the world.\u00a0 However, on days like today, I am reminded of the value of “reality communities” and traditions that have\u00a0endured for thousands of years.\u00a0\u00a0I am\u00a0also reminded that new calendars and new\u00a0ways to communicate\u00a0aren’t\u00a0always better than the old ones.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
