On May 2, the Ambler Theater was transformed into a family celebration.\u00a0 Kids laughed at the Great Holtzie, danced to Alex and the Kaleidoscope Band and were mesmerized by Michael Bonacci’s magic.\u00a0 We\u00a0even spotted a few parents hiding out in the theaters watching classic Looney Toons\u00a0and Sports Bloopers.<\/span><\/p>\n

Fun was a key ingredient of the day, but so was saving more lives.\u00a0 To that end, we raised over $80,000.\u00a0 At Simon’s Fund, we convert dollars raised into lives saved.\u00a0 It costs $50 for us to screen a child’s heart.\u00a0 For every 100 children we screen, one will\u00a0discover\u00a0a potentially fatal heart defect.\u00a0 As a result of Simon’s Day, we can screen 1,600 more children and save\u00a0sixteen more lives.<\/p>\n

Five years ago, we tried to turn our tragedy into hope.\u00a0 Now, five years later, it’s a reality.\u00a0 Thanks to everyone for\u00a0their generosity, and for helping us to save one child’s life . . . and then another by raising awareness about conditions that lead to sudden cardiac arrest and death.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
