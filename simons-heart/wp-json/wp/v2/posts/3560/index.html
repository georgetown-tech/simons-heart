The very first Simon’s Day was held at the World Cafe Live on Simon’s first birthday (October 21, 2006). \u00a0At that event, 80 friends (adults and kids) listened to the music of Erin Flynn. \u00a0We also had a mini-heart screening. \u00a0We came together to remember Simon. \u00a0A few years later, Simon’s Day became our annual fundraiser. \u00a0We invited families, not just adults, to help us raise money to check hearts and save lives.<\/p>\n

On March 13, 2012, we came full circle. \u00a0We abandoned the fundraising component, and gathered families for an entertainment lalapalooza for kids, just because. \u00a0We wanted to celebrate together. \u00a0We wanted to remember Simon together. \u00a0We wanted to raise awareness together.<\/p>\n

At Simon’s Day, over 300 people gathered at the Ambler Theater to be entrained by the Great Holtzie (comedian), John Pizzi (ventriloquist) and Scott Alexander (magician). They danced with the Phillie Phanatic, who also got an ECG exam. \u00a0The entered a heart art contest, got airbrush tattoos, decorated heart cookies and ate lots and lots of food. \u00a0They watched basketball and concerts on the big screens. \u00a0They met students that discovered heart conditions at Simon’s Fund heart screenings.<\/p>\n

We’re lucky that we can take moment to just celebrate with families that benefit from our mission. \u00a0Of course, we need to raise money to continue our good work, but for that day . . . Simon’s Day . . . we just smiled.<\/p>\n
