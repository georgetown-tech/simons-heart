By Margo Rutledge Kissell, Staff Writer Updated 1:48 AM Friday, September 23, 2011<\/p>\n

DAYTON \u2014 Two months before 9-year-old Unique Figgers collapsed at Westwood PreK-8 School and died Tuesday, Dayton Public Schools laid off 10 school nurses \u2014 one-third of its nursing staff.<\/p>\n

Among them was the full-time nurse at Westwood, which now has one nurse splitting her days between the elementary school and Thurgood Marshall High School. \u00a0On Tuesday morning, when Unique collapsed after reportedly telling friends she felt dizzy, the school nurse was at Belmont High School as part of a team performing vision screenings.<\/p>\n

Though the cause of death still has not been determined as investigators await test results following Wednesday\u2019s autopsy, the girl\u2019s sudden death has put a spotlight on the nursing staff reductions and sparked concern among some who wonder whether having a school nurse on duty that day might have changed the outcome.<\/p>\n
