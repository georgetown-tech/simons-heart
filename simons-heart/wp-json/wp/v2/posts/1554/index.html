<\/a>As Americans, we will spend approximately $15 billion on gifts for Valentine’s Day.\u00a0 This statistic,\u00a0coupled with the fact that\u00a0a calendar\u00a0mandates\u00a0we\u00a0declare our love for someone, makes it easy to hate this holiday.\u00a0 It’s commercial, it’s fabricated and it’s contrived.<\/p>\n

The\u00a0real\u00a0Valentine’s Day\u00a0commemorates\u00a0several Christian martyrs\u00a0from Ancient Rome.\u00a0 The romance thing didn’t enter the picture until\u00a0Courtly Love dominated the Middle Ages.\u00a0 This is more interesting to me, particularly given the recent events of my life.<\/p>\n

When I combine this heart-themed holiday with the concept of martyrdom, I have stumbled upon a meaningful holiday.\u00a0 I reflect on the thousands of kids that die every year from sudden cardiac arrest.\u00a0 Their deaths are tragic, but in the context of my new-found holiday, they are martyrs.\u00a0 From their deaths, we can raise awareness, change policies and eventually save lives.<\/p>\n
