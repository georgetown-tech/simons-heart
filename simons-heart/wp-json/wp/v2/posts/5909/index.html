Heart Month may be coming to an end, but the battle to raise awareness about sudden cardiac arrest and death in children is a year-round effort.\u00a0 The American Academy of Pediatrics estimates that two thousand U.S. children die every year from sudden cardiac arrest.\u00a0 A study from the Mayo Clinic shows that it is responsible for up to 15% of all sudden infant deaths (SIDS).\u00a0 Furthermore, the AHA recently published a study showing that sudden cardiac arrest isn\u2019t so sudden \u2013 there are warning signs.<\/p>\n

Armed with this information, Simon\u2019s Fund, a Plymouth Meeting based nonprofit organization founded in memory of Simon Sudman, a seemingly healthy baby boy who died from sudden cardiac arrest, teamed up with the Pennsylvania Chapter of the American Academy of Pediatrics, to produce a full color poster for every pediatric office in the Commonwealth.\u00a0 The poster campaign educates parents about the warning signs of conditions that lead to sudden cardiac arrest, like fainting during exercise, which is the #1 warning sign.\u00a0 The posters were created and paid for by Simon\u2019s Fund.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are very excited to be working with Simon\u2019s Fund on this campaign. It is our hope that these posters will supplement the efforts of our pediatricians to educate patients and families about sudden cardiac arrest,\u201d says Allen Nussbaum, MD, FAAP, President of the PA Chapter of the AAP.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis project fits perfectly within our mission,\u201d says Darren Sudman, Executive Director and Co-Founder of Simon\u2019s Fund.\u00a0 We need to make sudden cardiac arrest a household term so that students, parents and coaches appreciate the threat of sudden cardiac arrest and recognize the symptoms.\u00a0 This bright and engaging poster will deliver that information in a very positive and upbeat tone.\u201d<\/p>\n
