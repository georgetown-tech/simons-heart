I’ve been to so many conferences.\u00a0 They ranged in content from\u00a0bankruptcy law to\u00a0social marketing to small businesses.\u00a0 At these conferences, I get the opportunity to meet\u00a0lots of different people.\u00a0 Most of the discussion occurs around the breakfast or lunch table.\u00a0 Typically, they\u00a0are strangers when we randomly sit down together, and most of\u00a0the time, they become\u00a0strangers again when we leave the conference.\u00a0\u00a0We pass the time talking about\u00a0how we slept, how late we stayed out and how busy or bad things are back\u00a0at the\u00a0office.<\/p>\n

This conference, hosted by\u00a0Parent Heart Watch, is very different.\u00a0\u00a0Here, the small talk comes second, if at all.\u00a0 The opener is usually something like “did you loose a child” or “how did your child die.”\u00a0 It quickly progresses to “what can we do about it so that it does not happen to another family.”<\/p>\n

In a world where we talk so much and never really say anything; where we do much but never really get much accomplished; it is inspiring to see people with broken lives come together to repair their lives by helping others.\u00a0 I appreciate the big talk and big action.\u00a0 Kudos to all the suffering and selfless people affiliated with Parent Heart Watch<\/a>.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
