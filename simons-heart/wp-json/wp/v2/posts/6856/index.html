This is sad.\u00a0 Do we really need to litigate for change?\u00a0 The following article shows that of the 108 deaths of NCAA athletes from 1982 – 2008 (documented by the National Center for Catastrophic Sports Injury Research), 65% of them were heart related.\u00a0 This means that they could have been prevented by a simple and cheap ECG exam.\u00a0 The NCAA chooses not to screen the hearts of athletes.<\/p>\n

However, the NCAA was recently for sued over the death of an athlete from a genetic sickle cell trait.\u00a0 Some schools screen for this – Rice University did not.\u00a0 As a result of the lawsuit, the NCAA tests for this\u00a0sickle cell trait.\u00a0 The sickle cell trait was responsible for 10% of deaths during this same period (1982 – 2008).\u00a0 Like an ECG exam, the test for sickle cell is inexpensive ($10).<\/p>\n

Do we really have to litigate for change or can we just take practical and inexpensive measures to protect the lives of our young athletes?<\/p>\n

By the way, how is this for a stat . . . Italy screens the hearts of all of its young athletes.\u00a0 They have reduced the incidents of sudden cardiac death by\u00a089%.<\/p>\n
