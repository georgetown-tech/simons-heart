(Reuters Health) – Screening athletes for hidden heart problems may not help prevent sudden deaths, a new study from Israel finds.<\/p>\n

The findings, published in the Journal of the American College of Cardiology, cast doubts on the usefulness of widespread, mandatory screening of young athletes with electrocardiography, or ECG.<\/p>\n

The test measures the electrical activity of the heart and can detect disturbances that might spell trouble.<\/p>\n

Mandatory screening has been in place in Italy<\/a> and Israel for years, while in the U.S. there has been some debate over whether to launch similar policies.<\/p>\n
