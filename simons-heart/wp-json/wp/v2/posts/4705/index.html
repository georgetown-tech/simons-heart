Today marks the one year anniversary of the signing of the Sudden Cardiac Arrest Prevention Act (Act 59)<\/a>. This is the only law in the country that protects student athletes from sudden cardiac arrest. It’s really cool to get a law passed, and even cooler to speak at the bill signing, but what kind of impact has this law had on student athletes? How much safer are student athletes today?<\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
<\/a><\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
The National Federation of State High School Associations<\/a> conducts an annual participation survey. During the 2011- 12 school year, 7.7 million students participated in high school sports. Pennsylvania ranked 6th with 318,000 high school students participating.<\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
IMPACT #1<\/strong>: Over 300,000 student athletes and their parents now know that sudden cardiac arrest is the #1 cause of death of student athletes.<\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
IMPACT #2<\/strong>: Tens of thousands of coaches were trained on the warning signs of sudden cardiac arrest. and were required to remove players that exhibited these warning signs.<\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
IMPACT #3<\/strong>: These videos, created by the Philadelphia Six<\/a> (NCAA Hoops coaches), Olympic Gold Medalist Dana Vollmer<\/a>, and NBA Champion Ronny Turiaf<\/a>, have been seen by thousands of coaches and players.<\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
IMPACT #4<\/strong>: Sanjay Gupta<\/a> introduced millions of people across the country to this law and the risks of sudden cardiac arrest for student athletes.<\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
IMPACT #5<\/strong>: Six other states have introduced this law: Illinois, Indiana, Massachusetts, Michigan, New Jersey and Oklahoma.<\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
Our goal is for a majority of states to pass a version of this law by 2015. Will you help us make that a reality? Email us<\/a> to find out what’s happening in your state.<\/div>\n
 <\/div>\n
