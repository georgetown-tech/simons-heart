Simon’s Fund and CHOP<\/p><\/div>\n

On October 30, Simon’s Fund and The Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia conducted a heart screening for the students of Norristown.\u00a0 We screened 227 hearts.\u00a0 All students received an ECG exam, had their blood pressure taken twice, and had their height and weight checked.\u00a0 There were thirteen students that received additional onsite testing – an\u00a0echocardiogram.\u00a0 When an ECG report\u00a0appears to be\u00a0abnomal, an echocardiogram is ordered.<\/p>\n

We’ve held screenings at seven different schools.\u00a0\u00a0This one was very special.\u00a0 This one was in memory of Akhir “Geedy” Frazier<\/a>, who was supposed to attend Norristown High School this year as a junior.\u00a0 Akhir collapsed while\u00a0playing\u00a0basketball at the Hank Gathers Rec Center in August.\u00a0 He died four days later in the hospital.<\/p>\n

There was an incredible vibe that existed at Norristown High School.\u00a0 It shone through the parents that were incredibly friendly and so appreciative; the students that were engaging; and the administrative staff and community leaders that were present and committed\u00a0throughout the day.\u00a0 Norristown showed us that it is a community.<\/p>\n

We started setting up at 7 AM.\u00a0 We left at 6 PM.\u00a0 We were drained.\u00a0 Not from all of the work.\u00a0 From the genuine emotions that we shared throughout the day.\u00a0 Like I said before, it was special.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
