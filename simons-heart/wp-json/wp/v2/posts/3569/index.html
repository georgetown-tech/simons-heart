It’s 10:50 PM, the night before our second Downingtown screening. \u00a0We’ll screen about 300 kids. \u00a0Three of them will likely find a heart condition. \u00a0This is the normal routine. \u00a0Review email folder for cancellations. \u00a0Update registration lists. \u00a0Make corrections to folders and forms. \u00a0Load up the van with equipment.<\/p>\n

However, this time around, the cancellations made me pause because of the reasons that some of the parents provided. \u00a0In the past, I probably wasn’t given a reason – just a “sorry can’t make it.” \u00a0The reasons prove to me that we have so much work to do to about raising awareness about the risks of sudden cardiac arrest and death.<\/p>\n

It went something like this . . . my child can no longer attend the screening because his\/her game got rescheduled and he\/she cannot miss the game. \u00a0How does that strike you?<\/p>\n

What does this analogy do to your perspective? \u00a0Your child has a lacrosse tournament in Florida on Sunday. \u00a0The flight leaves tomorrow. \u00a0The airline tells you that the plane is 7 – 10 years old and the engine has never been inspected. \u00a0The airline wants to check the engine, but that means the flight will be delayed and your child will miss the tournament. \u00a0You convince the airline to check the engine on Monday.<\/p>\n

I’m pretty sure that most parents are not putting their child on that plane, even though the likelihood of anyone dying in a plane crash is\u00a01:11,000,000.<\/a>\u00a0 By conservative accounts, the likelihood of a child dying from sudden cardiac arrest is 1:300,000<\/a>.<\/p>\n

I’m not judging. \u00a0I’ve declined to participate in lots of stuff that others deem important. \u00a0To me, it was just a lesser priority. \u00a0I’m suggesting that this shouldn’t be. \u00a0Getting your child’s heart checked needs to be more important than participating in a regular season game. \u00a0It should actually be part of the pre-participation sports physical.<\/p>\n

I’m realizing that I need to do more to raise awareness so that parents can appreciate this real threat.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
