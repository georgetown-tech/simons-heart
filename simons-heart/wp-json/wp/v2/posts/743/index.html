For five years, all of the celebrations in my life have been played\u00a0against the backdrop of tragedy.\u00a0\u00a0 That doesn’t mean that\u00a0all of the celebrations have been bad.\u00a0\u00a0It just means that every\u00a0celebration\u00a0is somehow dampened by the\u00a0loss of Simon.<\/p>\n

Today, was an exception.\u00a0 Today, I\u00a0saw the other side of life.\u00a0\u00a0I left my world and visited another world.\u00a0 I\u00a0got to attend Melissa Fair’s\u00a0 bat mitzvah.\u00a0 She looked amazing.\u00a0 She sang and chanted beautifully.\u00a0 She spoke with confidence and compassion.\u00a0 She rose above the occasion.<\/p>\n

Melissa attended a Simon’s Day heart screening two years ago.\u00a0 The screening revealed a congenital heart defect that required open heart surgery.\u00a0 As she put it today, “now, I’m better than ever.”<\/p>\n

Melissa asked Phyllis and I to participate in her bat mitzvah celebration.\u00a0 We had a part in the service.\u00a0 We lit a candle at the reception.\u00a0 We sat at a table with Lori and Ken (parents) and their siblings.\u00a0 I realized very quickly that we were the only non-family member in the service and at the table.\u00a0\u00a0Then, I realized that we were their family too.<\/p>\n

We\u00a0know that the activities of Simon’s Fund affect other people’s lives.\u00a0 That’s our objective.\u00a0 But, we don’t really know to what extent, and we don’t pause for too long to find out.\u00a0 There’s more to do.<\/p>\n

Today was a very long pause.\u00a0 Today,\u00a0I\u00a0heard about how\u00a0frightening and\u00a0uncertain those days of surgery were for the family.\u00a0 Today,\u00a0I learned about how much of an\u00a0effect the surgery had on Melissa’s life and character.\u00a0 Today,\u00a0I finally realized how much of an impact that Simon had on another life, and\u00a0how appreciated he is by her family and friends.<\/p>\n

I cried alot today.\u00a0Typically, this amount of crying\u00a0is reserved for\u00a0anniversaries, birthdays, or missed milestones.\u00a0 Today was different.\u00a0\u00a0Today, I cried for the joy that Simon left\u00a0behind, not for the sorrow of him leaving.\u00a0\u00a0I cried over the happiness of what I was seeing, not over the despair of what I was missing.\u00a0 I cried over the pride of knowing that Simon’s life touched someone else’s life, not over the agony of him losing his own life.<\/p>\n

As\u00a0Melissa called us up to light a candle today,\u00a0she said in her poem that “we saved her life.”\u00a0 Melissa, thank you, but\u00a0the truth is, today your family changed mine.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
