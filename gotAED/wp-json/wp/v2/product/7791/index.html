Stryker Sports Complex would love to own a AED in case an emergency arises. In the United States, a young competitive athlete dies suddenly of Sudden Cardiac Death every three days. Young athletes are more than twice as likely to experience SCD than young non-athletes (Close the Gap). We are aware that AED’s strengthen the chain of survival. They can restore a normal heart rhythm in victims of sudden cardiac arrest.The American College of Cardiology notes that, \u201cThe most important contributing factor for survival of sudden cardiac arrest is the time from collapse to defibrillation. Survival decreases 10% every minute until a shock is applied.\u201d <\/p>\n
